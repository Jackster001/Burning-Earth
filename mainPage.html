What Is Climte Change?

Climate Change is when the earth changes its weather patterns at the global and regional level. Since the 20th century, carbon dioxide and methane levels have increased due to human activities such as burning fossil fuels, fracking, deforestation and more. Greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide and methane traps heat emitted from the sun. This heat is then released onto the Earth, hence the reason why the Earth is becoming warmer. Since the industrial revolution, greenhouse gases have increased to astronomical levels not seen in 3 million years.

Evidence




Many scientist and researchers in the world have agreed and shown evidence the Earth has been warming up. Its been recorded that the global temperature have risen by about 1.4 degree fahrenheit since 1880. The United States alone have experienced an average increase of 11 degrees fahrenheit. Many climate change experts agree that green house gases trap heat on earth, which contributes to the rise in temperature. The rise in temperature leads the melting of ice caps in alaska and antartica, which increase the frequency of severity of tropical storms. The increase in temperature also leads to forests drying up which is the reason why there are many wildfires during the summer and fall season. Learn More





Causes of Climate Change

Green House Gases

Greenhouse gases are gases that trap heat in the Earth's Atmosphere. 82% of green house gases is cardon dioxide which enters into the atmosphere through burning of fossil fuels, tree and other biological materials. To remove this gas from the atmosphere we need plants and trees to absorb this gas.









Deforestation

Because of the rise in temperature and many tropical storms, rainforest and wildlife experienced an increase in planet growth which as a result decreases the water and moisture in the ecosystem. As these forest gets dry, there will be many instances of wildfires. The decrease in forest and trees will lead to more cardon emissions and less oxygen.




Fracking

Fracking is when a drilling device is used to drill into Earth to extract gas and oil. The chemical that is released from fracking is Methane which is 25 times more effective than carbondioxide in trapping heat in the atmostphere. This release of methane is directly related to the increase of air pollution around the area.
